The Good News of Jesus Christ

Clear, scripture-centered, and for everyone—because God shows no partiality.

1) The Problem

“All have sinned and fall short of the glory of God.” (Romans 3:23)

“The wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord.” (Romans 6:23)

We have all sinned. Sin separates us from God and leads to eternal death.

2) Condemnation Without Christ

“He who believes in Him is not condemned; but he who does not believe is condemned already, because he has not believed in the name of the only begotten Son of God.” (John 3:18)

“It is appointed for men to die once, but after this the judgment.” (Hebrews 9:27)

Without Christ, we stand guilty before a holy God, facing judgment.

3) God Shows No Partiality

“For there is no partiality with God.” (Romans 2:11)

“Then Peter opened his mouth and said: ‘In truth I perceive that God shows no partiality. But in every nation whoever fears Him and works righteousness is accepted by Him.’” (Acts 10:34–35)

God is not a respecter of persons. No one is beyond His reach. Salvation is offered to all.

4) God’s Love and Provision

“But God demonstrates His own love toward us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us.” (Romans 5:8)

“Christ died for our sins according to the Scriptures, and that He was buried, and that He rose again the third day according to the Scriptures.” (1 Corinthians 15:3–4)

God sent His Son, Jesus, to die in our place and rise again, so that we could be forgiven and made right with Him.

5) The Free Gift of Salvation

“For by grace you have been saved through faith, and that not of yourselves; it is the gift of God, not of works, lest anyone should boast.” (Ephesians 2:8–9)

Salvation cannot be earned by good works — it is received only by faith in Jesus Christ.

6) The Urgent Call

“Repent therefore, and turn back, that your sins may be blotted out.” (Acts 3:19)

“Behold, now is the accepted time; behold, now is the day of salvation.” (2 Corinthians 6:2)

“If you confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus and believe in your heart that God has raised Him from the dead, you will be saved.” (Romans 10:9)

God calls you to turn from sin and believe in Jesus today. Tomorrow is not promised.

7) The Promise of Life in Christ

“There is therefore now no condemnation to those who are in Christ Jesus.” (Romans 8:1)

“For God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have everlasting life.” (John 3:16)

Through Christ, you can be forgiven, free from condemnation, and have eternal life with God.
